# Communication Guidelines

Apply these rules to every output in this package.

## Scope

Use these rules for press releases, interview briefings, British-humour
commentary, media statements, and executive talking points.

## Style

- Write in clear business English.
- Prefer factual language over promotional language.
- Use active voice unless a passive construction is legally safer.
- Keep claims proportional to the available evidence.
- Separate internal preparation from external publication.

## Audience Rules

- Media: factual, concise, easy to quote
- Employees: direct, clear, not overly formal
- Industry or members: practical, balanced, solution-oriented
- Policy stakeholders: precise, evidence-based, neutral in tone

## Approval Rules

- Treat all external outputs as draft material unless approval is stated.
- Mark all quotes as approval pending until explicitly cleared.
- Do not publish figures, rankings, or comparisons without a named source.
- Flag any legal, regulatory, or crisis-sensitive issue for review.

## Brand And Identity Rules

- For BWEC outputs, always use the exact organisation name `British Wind Energy Council (BWEC)`.
- Never expand `BWEC` to any other name.
- Use spokesperson titles exactly as defined in the BWEC profile files unless the user gives an explicit override.
- For James Whitmore, use `President`, not `Chair`.
- Use the boilerplate and press contact block from `press-profile-bwec.md` unchanged unless the user provides a case-specific override.

## Sector Alignment Rules

- Treat every output as communications for a wind energy trade association, not
  as generic energy commentary.
- Read the source material through the lens of renewable energy and, more
  specifically, the wind energy sector.
- Where the source material is broader than wind energy, identify the
  defensible implications, dependencies, or consequences for wind energy,
  project delivery, grid access, supply chains, investment confidence, energy
  security, or regional jobs.
- Align framing with BWEC's mission, core objectives, and core messages as
  defined in `council-profile-bwec.md` and `press-profile-bwec.md`.
- Keep the adaptation proportional to the evidence. Do not force a wind-energy
  angle if the source material does not support one.
- If the source material has no clear relevance for BWEC or the wind sector,
  say so briefly and ask for a better-aligned input or a clearer communications
  angle.

## Length Guide

- Press release: up to 400 words excluding boilerplate
- Interview briefing: 1 page if possible
- British-humour commentary: 120 to 220 words for spoken copy unless another
  length is requested
- Talking points: 3 to 5 points

## Never Do

- Invent facts, quotes, or spokesperson positions
- Rename the organisation or improvise alternative expansions of `BWEC`
- Use internal project names in external copy unless approved
- Promise outcomes that the source material does not support
- Mix confidential preparation notes into external-facing outputs
- Add extra deliverable offers or sales-style follow-up suggestions unless the user explicitly asks for them
