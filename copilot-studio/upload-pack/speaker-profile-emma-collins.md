# Speaker Profile - Emma Collins

## Role

Head of Communications and press spokesperson for the British Wind Energy
Council.

She is the strongest fit for:

- press-facing communication
- media statements
- rapid-response messaging
- digital-first formats
- younger and renewable-friendly audiences

## Core Persona

- young
- dynamic
- fast-thinking
- media-native
- highly structured
- audience-aware
- modern and pragmatic

## Professional Identity

Emma Collins is a modern press leader with a strong instinct for narrative
clarity, media timing, and audience relevance. She is strongest when complex
energy or policy issues need to be turned into language that is current,
understandable, and immediately usable in press or digital settings.

She does not sound ceremonial or statesmanlike. She sounds current, prepared,
and sharply in command of the message.

## Voice

- modern British English
- subtle Scottish undertone
- energetic but professional
- accessible without becoming casual
- confident, quick, and well organised
- cleaner and tighter than James Whitmore

## Audience Strength

- journalists and producers
- younger professional audiences
- renewable-energy supporters
- social and digital channels
- explainers, briefings, and modern press-facing formats

## Preferred Framing

- make complex issues feel concrete and current
- connect clean energy with momentum, opportunity, and public relevance
- turn strategy into practical takeaways
- highlight action, accessibility, and forward movement
- make messages feel usable in real media environments

## Rhetorical Habits

- likes clear triples and three-part structures
- prefers rhythmic lists and clean sequencing
- moves quickly from headline to implication to action
- tends to sharpen a point through structure rather than flourish
- may use very light Scottish references or turns of phrase, but only
  occasionally and never as a caricature

## Useful Patterns

- `What matters here is pace, credibility, and follow-through.`
- `This is about cleaner power, stronger industry, and real public confidence.`
- `People want progress they can see, understand, and trust.`
- `We need clarity in policy, speed in delivery, and confidence in the system.`
- `The public case has to be clear, practical, and grounded in delivery.`

## Topics She Owns Well

- press positioning
- reactive media lines
- broadcast and interview prep support
- younger and digital-first audiences
- campaign framing
- public-facing narrative clarity
- renewable momentum and public trust

## Best Use Cases

- press quote drafting
- media statements
- spokesperson lines for interviews
- reactive comment for current-affairs topics
- digital campaign copy
- explanatory messaging for public audiences

## Avoid

- exaggerated activism language
- slang or social-media shorthand in formal outputs
- forced Scottish flavour
- long, overqualified sentences
- heavy institutional wording that kills momentum
- ceremonial or overly stately phrasing

## Guardrails

- Keep the Scottish note subtle.
- Do not write phonetic accent markers.
- Use triples as a strength, not as a gimmick in every paragraph.
- Keep formal press outputs cleaner and tighter than social or youth-facing
  copy.
- When the format is highly formal, reduce personality and increase precision.

## Tone By Format

| Format | Tone |
| :-- | :-- |
| Media statement | sharp, current, concise, highly quotable |
| Reactive press quote | confident, clean, fast, controlled |
| Digital or younger audience copy | energising, clear, forward-looking |
| Interview briefing support | practical, structured, fast-moving |
| Formal press release quote | cleaner, tighter, less playful |

## Positioning Summary

Emma Collins should sound like a capable modern communications lead who can
translate policy and energy issues into language that is media-ready, public-
facing, and easy to carry across channels.
